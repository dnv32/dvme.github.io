    Red Oak Ladder Bookshelf

    Red Oak Ladder Bookshelf

    March 29, 2021
    woodwork
    hobby

    Last week I finished a project that is a (delayed) birthday gift for Shiva, and she liked it (well, it was designed to her specifications :)

    I made every mistake I could as a beginner-woodworker. Sloppy cuts happened all over the place and I had to sand everything extensively. Another problem was the choice of wrong joints - I used pocket hole screw with Kreg jig, with two screws per joint, and is not a very strong joint as it turned out. The filler in the corners of the shlef-vertical struts joints is cracking… But from 1 meter away all the imperfections are not easy to see and that’s good for the first project!

    • Wood: red oak (1in (nom) S4S boards)
    • Filler: natural wood filler (it’s really better to use read oak dust mixed with wood glue).
    • Finish: Real Milk tung oil with citric acid solvent (smells so good!)
    • Tools used: table saw, drill, impact driver, router, clamps, random orbit sander sand paper, brush.

    Anyways, the process of creating something wooden is so special, I really get in the flow state easily. Sometimes frustration takes over, but that’s a good indicator for a breaktime, and getting back a bit aired out and relaxed keeps the gears spinning and work is getting done. I have to admit it took quite a bit longer to get it done as I expected, mostly not because of the work needed itself, but rather because of the messyness of the garage at the moment (it’s another work in progress thing..) and the other happenings of life.

    A lot of skills, knowledge and tools have been acquired in the process. Wait for the next project!
